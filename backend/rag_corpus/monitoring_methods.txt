Photo-quadrats and structure-from-motion orthomosaics are common for repeating percent cover estimates. Automated semantic segmentation can speed annotation but should be validated against expert labels, especially for rare classes or boundary pixels.

The Coralscapes benchmark targets dense labeling of reef imagery with dozens of fine-grained classes. For communication with stakeholders, fine classes are often merged into live coral, bleached coral, dead coral, algae, and substrate groups.

When comparing model-derived percentages between sites, keep camera altitude, lighting, and water clarity consistent; these strongly affect apparent cover and class confusion.
